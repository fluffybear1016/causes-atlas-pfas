Babies of PFAS-exposed mothers

2.1× more eczema by their second birthday.

In girls, mothers with higher prenatal PFOA: 2.1× the rate of atopic dermatitis. PFNA: 2.22×. This is what shows up on your baby's skin.

Chen et al. 2018, Environ Health · PMCID PMC5773146

Miami-Dade childhood cancer, ages 0–14
+2.2%/yr

Rising in Miami-Dade while the rest of the country falls. Miami is 30% higher than the U.S. average. Already.

NCI State Cancer Profiles, 2018–2022 · SCP code 516

Pediatric thyroid cancer · U.S.

+4.6%/yr — six times faster than the 1975 rate.

South Florida is one of only two statistically significant thyroid cancer clusters in the entire state of Florida. Mechanism: PFAS displaces thyroid hormone from the proteins that carry it through your child's blood.

Amin et al. 2014 · PMID 24868551 · NCI State Cancer Profiles

Miami-Dade babies born underweight

1 in 12 Miami-Dade babies is born underweight.

Black infants 14.0%. White infants 6.5%. PFOA crosses the placenta — what's in mom's blood ends up in the cord at a ratio of 0.87. Apelberg 2007 confirmed it in 293 Baltimore mother-baby pairs.

March of Dimes PeriStats · Apelberg 2007 EHP · PMID 18008002

South Florida pregnancies vs. the U.S.
9.4%

Of South Florida pregnancies develop dangerous blood pressure complications. More than double the U.S. average of 4.2%.

Memorial Healthcare System (Broward), October 2022 · CBS Miami

Miscarriage · weeks 12–22

Higher PFAS in mom's blood. Higher miscarriage.

220 miscarriages versus 218 live births, with maternal blood collected before the pregnancy ended. Multiple PFAS positively associated with miscarriage in the second trimester.

Liew et al. 2020, Environ Health Perspect · PMID 32319790

Miami-Dade infant mortality · 2013 to 2023
+9%

Miami-Dade infant mortality has risen 9% in ten years. While the U.S. rate stayed flat.

March of Dimes PeriStats · Miami-Dade County

Daughters · puberty timing & bone density

Higher PFAS pushed everything later — first breasts, first period, first bone density.

PFAS in mom's blood is associated with later puberty, lower estradiol, and lower bone density in girls. NHANES confirmed lower bone density in U.S. women across all ages.

Roth et al. 2025 · Khalil et al. 2016 EHP · PMID 26058082

You can't see PFAS. You can't taste it. You can't boil it out.

The only thing that works is filtration certified by NSF/ANSI 53 (carbon block + ion exchange) or 58 (reverse osmosis) at the point of use. Bottled water is not the answer — most bottled water contains PFAS too.
